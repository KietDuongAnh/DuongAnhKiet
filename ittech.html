ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

What is Artificial Intelligence?

“Artificial intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think like humans and mimic their actions. The term may also be applied to any machine that exhibits traits associated with a human mind such as learning and problem-solving.” (How Artificial Intelligence Works, 2021)[2]. In short, as a broad field of computer science, artificial intelligence (AI) aims to give machines the capabilities to accomplish tasks that normally require human intellectual capacity.

State of the art of AI

Machine learning (ML):

Machine learning is a subset of artificial intelligence that uses analytic techniques to give machines the ability to handle data without specific instructions. It is possible for systems, in particular, to learn from statistics, recognize patterns, and make decisions with limited human involvement. Machine learning today is more powerful due to more advanced computing technologies. In the past, it grew out of pattern recognition and the concept that computers can learn without being programmed to do specific tasks. As a result of iterative nature, computers are able to learn from supplied data and use them to make decisions to independently adapt. Machine learning has become more popular in modern society due to algorithms that allow computers to perform more sophisticated calculations on big data and run faster than ever before. For example, we can see its application in Netflix's online suggestion promotions or self-driving car supported by Google.

Deep learning (DL):

“Deep learning takes inspiration from the activity of neurons within the brain to learn how to recognize complex patterns through learned data. This is thanks to the use of algorithms, mainly statistical calculations. The word ‘deep’ refers to the large number of levels of neurons that ML models simultaneously, which helps acquire rich representations of data to obtain performance gains.” (Artificial Intelligence: state of the art - Thinkopen, 2021)[3]. Specifically, deep learning employs massive neural networks with lots of layers of processors to learn complicated patterns in huge amounts of data, taking advantage of modern in computing power and enhanced training methods. In today’s world, deep learning application has evolved significantly, especially in human-to-machine interaction. Gesture, touch and voice recognition technology, for example, is replacing the mouse and the keyboard in computers.

Natural Language Processing (NPL):

“Natural language processing (NLP) is a branch of artificial intelligence that helps computers understand, interpret and manipulate human language. NLP draws from many disciplines, including computer science and computational linguistics, in its pursuit to fill the gap between human communication and computer understanding.”(What is Natural Language Processing?, 2021)[4]. Accordingly, natural language processing refers to the capacity of computers to analysis, comprehend, and produce human language, including speech. Natural language processing encompasses a wide range of techniques for translating human language. NLP tasks, in general, decompose language into smaller pieces, attempt to understand relationships between the parts, and investigate how the pieces come together to produce meaning. In practice, there are many NPL applications that are used but we may not recognize. For example, when we traverse a website by using its constructed search engine or by picking a recommended topic, we have used NLP techniques for search and content classification.

Why is it important?

  • Learning by repetition: AI performs a large number of tasks. By virtue of its recursive nature, artificial intelligence (AI) is able to discover through data reliably without tiredness.
  • Adaptation: Artificial intelligence (AI) adapts through the use of progressive learning algorithms. AI identifies patterns, structure in data so that algorithms can learn by itself, and adapt when given new data.
  • More accuracy and deeper analyzing: Deep learning models are taught to learn directly from data. As a result of today's incredible computing power and big data, AI can analyze deeper data using multi-layered neural networks with incredible accuracy.
  • Integration of AI in products: AI enhances the intelligence of existing products. Many of the products we use have been enhanced with AI capabilities, similar to how Siri was added as a feature to Apple products. Furthermore, bots and intelligent systems are now being combined with massive amounts of data to improve a wide range of technologies.

CLOUD COMPUTING

What is Cloud Computing?

“Simply put, cloud computing is the delivery of computing services—including servers, storage, databases, networking, software, analytics, and intelligence—over the Internet (“the cloud”) to offer faster innovation, flexible resources, and economies of scale.” (What Is Cloud Computing? A Beginner’s Guide | Microsoft Azure, 2021) [5]. Cloud computing refers to the Internet-based delivery of a wide range of IT resources. Data storage, software, databases, and other computing resources are hosted at a remote server managed by a cloud services provider.

State of the art of Cloud computing service

In general, the structure of cloud computing service can be commonly divided into 3 layer: software layer, platform layer and infrastructure layer.

Software as a service (SaaS):

“Software as a service (SaaS) replaces the traditional on-device software with software that is licensed on a subscription basis. It is centrally hosted in the cloud.” (The Top 3 Cloud Computing Service Models, 2021) [6]. To be more specific, as a result of the SaaS, consumers can use applications that are hosted on a cloud infrastructure. It is possible to access the applications from a wide range of client devices, and the user is not in control of the cloud infrastructure. Cloud applications can take advantage of the automatic feature to achieve higher performance, accessibility, and reduced costs, which make cloud apps more convenient than the traditional ones. In addition, SaaS also provides customers a pay-as-you-go approach that means customers just pay for what they needs. This allows users to measure their application services as needed. In fact, we have already been using many SaaS approaches today without realization. Most SaaS apps, such as Facebook, YouTube, email, and Google apps, can be accessed immediately via a web browser, without the need to download or install them.

Platform as a service (PaaS):

The platform layer, which is on the top of the infrastructure layer, is made up of application frameworks. “PaaS provides the ability to create and manage custom cloud applications. It enables users to deploy their created or acquired applications using programming languages, frameworks and tools that are provided by the cloud host. The user doesn't manage or control the underlying cloud infrastructure (networks, servers, operating systems and storage), but does have control over deployed applications and possibly the application-hosting configurations.” (Cloud Computing - What it is and why it matters, 2021) [7]. Developers can build, manage and deploy their applications by using framework, programming languages and other supports from the cloud center. The platform layer's aim is to limit the burden of directly deploying applications. Developers can focus less on management and more on coding and application development. In practice, PaaS can simplify building process and speed up deployment, for example, Google App Engine offers API support for faster operating databases and storage of a website.

Infrastructure as a service (IaaS):

IaaS is the highest as well as the most basic level of the hierarchical model providing computing resources. “These can include storage, networks, processing and other general computing resources. The IaaS user can run software from the cloud; access operating systems, applications and frameworks; and perform the general administrative functions; but does not manage or control the underlying infrastructure.” (Cloud Computing - What it is and why it matters, 2021) [8]. IaaS is a crucial component as a pool to store data and other resources. This also makes it easy for businesses to append, remove, or modify IT infrastructure on the fly. Today, IaaS is becoming more popular among IT organizations because companies only pay for what they use when purchase IaaS instead of hardware.
